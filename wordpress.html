    What is WORDPRESS ?

    Word Press is a free and open-source content management system (CMS) based on PHP and MySQL. Word Press was born out of a craving for a delicate, well-architecture personal publishing system created on PHP and MYSQL and licensed under the GPLv2. It is a sophisticated and stable products.

    Word Press has grown to be the largest self-hosted blogging tool in the globe, used on millions of sites and seen by tens of millions of people every day. Word Press has evolved to be a full content management system with apparatus, plugins and customizable themes. Word Press is licensed under GPLv2, which lists terms and conditions for copying, adapting and apportion free software.

    HISTORY OF WORDPRESS:

    Word Press is fresh software, but its roots and advancement go back to 2001. It is a sophisticated and stable product. It was released on May 27, 2003, by its founders, Matt Mullenweg and Mike Little, as a fork of b2/cafelog. The license under which Word Press software is released is the GPLv2 (or later) from the Free Software Foundation. As of January 2015, more than 23.3% of the top 10 million websites now use Word Press. Word Press was used by more than 26.4% of the top 10 million websites as of April 2016. Word Press is the most popular blogging system in use on the Web, at more than 60 million websites.
